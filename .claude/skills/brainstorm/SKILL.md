# Brainstorm

`$ARGUMENTS` — optional: a broad topic or constraint.

Read `research/research-context.md` for expertise and lab capabilities. Read `research/problem-selection.md` for what makes a great problem. Read `research/problem-repository.md` for existing problem ideas. Grep `library/papers/*/summary.md` for topic-relevant keywords, focusing on "Notes & open questions" sections. Also grep `library/blogs/*/summary.md` — blogs are fair game as inspiration and signal here, even though they can't be cited in a final paper. Check `projects/*/plan.md` to avoid duplicating past work.

Generate 3–5 problem statements as draft `plan.md` files, ranked by feasibility × impact. Prefer problems where available tools give an unfair advantage.

Append any new problem ideas to `research/problem-repository.md`.
